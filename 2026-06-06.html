New

Ladybird will no longer accept public pull requests
New
Jun 5, 2026
Quoting Andreas Kling's "Changing How We Develop Ladybird": "A substantial patch used to imply substantial effort, and that effort was a reasonable proxy for good faith. Lands squarely in the curl/SQLite maintainer-overload theme: AI breaks the trust heuristics open source ran on.

Notable Themes

AI is dissolving open source's trust heuristics

Ladybird closing public PRs ("a substantial patch used to imply substantial effort… that assumption no longer holds") is the sharpest statement yet of a theme that's been building through curl, SQLite, and the AI-assisted-security-report flood. Patch size, effort, and good faith used to correlate; AI severed that link. O'Reilly's "Open Source Ecosystems" (open strategy vs. private tactics) and the VoidZero→Cloudflare consolidation circle the same question from the governance side: who is actually accountable for the code, and the projects, we all depend on?

Prompt-injection defense is shipping as product, not just advice

OpenAI's Lockdown Mode going live — cutting the exfiltration leg of Willison's Lethal Trifecta with deterministic, non-AI-evaluated controls — is the consumer counterpart to Anthropic's sandboxing docs and Simon's own MicroPython+WASM code sandbox. The industry is converging on the same insight: you can't fully prevent injection, so cut off exfiltration and contain execution. Defense is moving from blog-post best-practice to default platform feature.

AI cost rationalization is now the steady-state, not the headline

It's notable how routine this has become: Uber's $1,500/tool cap, Anthropic's Jun 15 Agent SDK credit-pool switch (covered again today), Microsoft pitching MAI models on lower developer cost, and KVarN/QKV research aimed at cheaper inference. The "experiment" framing is gone; token spend is a managed budget line and a model-design constraint.

"AI building AI" keeps accruing concrete artifacts

Karpathy's AutoResearch numbers (700 experiments / 2 days / 20 discovered optimizations) give the recursive-self-improvement debate a grassroots data point alongside Anthropic's Institute report, AlphaEvolve, and Claude Code's Dynamic Workflows. The framing has fully moved from demo to roadmap — and now to reproducible single-GPU tooling anyone can run.
