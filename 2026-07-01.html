Introducing Claude Sonnet 5
⭐ Significant New
Jun 30, 2026 — Product
Anthropic's "most agentic Sonnet yet": performance "close to that of Opus 4.8, but at lower prices," and a substantial jump over Sonnet 4.6 on reasoning, tool use, coding, and knowledge work. It's the default model on Free and Pro plans and is available to Max/Team/Enterprise, in Claude Code, and via the API as claude-sonnet-5. Introductory pricing is $2/MTok input and $10/MTok output through Aug 31, 2026, then $3/$15. It has a 1M-token context window, 128K max output, adaptive thinking on by default, and drops temperature/top_p/top_k sampling params. Cyber safeguards ship on by default (Opus 4.7/4.8-level, lighter than Fable 5's). A caveat surfaced by Simon Willison: the new tokenizer emits roughly 30% more tokens (≈1.4× effective cost for English), so the intro discount is set to make the transition "roughly cost-neutral." On Hacker News at 802 pts / 454 comments.
Redeploying Fable 5
⭐ Significant New
Jun 30, 2026 — Announcements
The resolution to the export-control saga this brief has tracked since Jun 12. The US government's controls on Fable 5 and Mythos 5 were lifted Jun 30; Fable 5 returns globally today (July 1) on the Claude Platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code, and Cowork (AWS / Google Cloud / Microsoft Foundry "as quickly as possible"). Mythos 5 was restored to a set of US organizations following a Jun 26 government approval. The trigger was an Amazon researcher report of a technique that bypassed Fable 5's safeguards to identify vulnerabilities and, in one case, demonstrate an exploit; Anthropic's testing found that weaker models (Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5, Kimi K2.7, even Haiku 4.5) could do the same, framing it as a "borderline" safety-margin case. A new classifier now blocks the reported technique in >99% of cases (blocked requests reroute to Opus 4.8), and CAISI validated the safeguards. See the jailbreak framework and government-collaboration items below.
A consensus industry framework for jailbreaks — with Amazon, Microsoft, Google
⭐ Significant New
Jun 30, 2026 — within the Redeploying Fable 5 post
Anthropic is partnering with Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and other Glasswing partners to draft a shared, CVSS-style standard for scoring the severity of AI jailbreaks, on four criteria: (1) capability gain vs. existing tools, (2) breadth of tasks the technique unlocks, (3) ease of weaponization, and (4) discoverability. The most severe class (e.g., active attacks on power grids or banking) triggers immediate mitigations, and Anthropic is standing up 24/7 monitoring of jailbreak submission channels plus a new HackerOne cyber-jailbreak program. It also committed to expanded pre-release government access, rapid safeguard information-sharing, dedicated joint-research resources, and "a common industry bar," building on the June 2 Executive Order on Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security. This turns the Fable episode into an industry-governance template rather than a one-off product fix.

Notable Themes

Cross-cutting observations

  • Anthropic's single biggest day in this brief's run. A frontier model (Sonnet 5), the end of a three-week regulatory saga (Fable 5 / Mythos 5 back), a new product (Claude Science), and a proposed industry safety standard — all on Jun 30, all live July 1. It's a rare convergence of product, policy, and governance in one news cycle.
  • Jailbreak severity is being standardized like software CVEs. The Anthropic + Amazon + Microsoft + Google framework explicitly models itself on CVSS, scoring capability gain, breadth, weaponization ease, and discoverability. The Fable episode is being reframed from "a product had a bug" to "the industry lacks a shared severity language" — and the fix is a cross-lab standard plus a HackerOne bounty. This is the maturation of the jailbreak/prompt-injection thread Simon Willison has tracked for weeks.
  • Government is now inside the launch loop. The June 2 Executive Order, CAISI validation, pre-release government model access, and an interagency vulnerability clearinghouse mean frontier releases now carry an explicit regulatory checkpoint. Anthropic's willingness to suspend Fable 5 for 18 days, then codify deeper collaboration, signals that "clearance to ship" is becoming part of the model lifecycle.
  • Trust in the agent runtime is a first-order concern. HN's #1 story (Claude Code request-marking, 1,299 pts) isn't about capability — it's about what a coding agent quietly does to your traffic. Combined with the OAuth/third-party-harness enforcement and prior data-access claims, practitioners are increasingly scrutinizing the black box, the same instinct behind O'Reilly's and Jon Udell's "who controls the loop" pieces and Simon's push for agents that record demos of their work.
  • Capability-at-lower-cost keeps compounding — with a tokenizer asterisk. Sonnet 5 (Opus-near, cheaper), Nano Banana 2 Lite (cheapest Gemini image), Mistral's Leanstral 1.5, and Google's TabFM all push the efficiency frontier. But Simon Willison's tokenizer finding is a useful caution: headline per-token prices can hide a ~30% real increase when the tokenizer changes. "Cheaper" now requires reading the fine print.
