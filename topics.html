Cross-industry AI safety standards — jailbreak severity & shared governance
The Fable 5 episode surfaced a structural gap: there's no consensus way to describe how severe an AI "jailbreak" is, so developers can't triage findings and governments have no agreed trigger for action. On Jun 30, Anthropic — together with Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and other Glasswing partners — proposed a shared, CVSS-style framework scoring a jailbreak on four axes: capability gain (how far beyond existing tools), breadth (how many offensive tasks it unlocks), ease of weaponization (human effort to turn it into an attack), and discoverability (how easy to obtain). The most severe class triggers immediate mitigations; Anthropic added 24/7 monitoring of submission channels and a HackerOne cyber-jailbreak bounty, and committed to expanded pre-release US-government access and shared safeguard information under the June 2 Executive Order. This reframes model safety from a per-product concern into an industry-governance standard, and is the maturation of the prompt-injection/jailbreak thread tracked elsewhere. Watch for: whether other labs (OpenAI, Mistral, Chinese labs) sign on, and whether the framework gets codified in regulation.

Loop engineering — from prompting agents to designing autonomous loops
